UNESCO

United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization

The UNESCO office in Bangkok was established in 1961. UNESCO is an agency of the United Nations (UN) with the purpose to contribute to peace and security by promoting international collaboration through educational, scientific, and cultural reforms in order to increase universal respect for justice, the rule of law, and human rights along with fundamental freedom proclaimed in the United Nations Charter.


FOSSASIA

FOSSASIA develops Open Source software and hardware for conversational AI, science and event management with a global developer community from its base in Asia. FOSSASIA organizes Open Technology events, and runs coding programs. The annual FOSSASIA Summit is a premier Open Technology event in Asia. FOSSASIA was founded in 2009.

A little more about UNESCO Techcul

UNESCO is launching TechCul to bring together tech and culture leaders with startups and entrepreneurs to develop win-win digital innovative technology solutions and business models to address the challenges faced in the culture and creative sector in times of a global pandemic.

UNESCO Techcul is an online program to develop technology ideas for the creative and culture sector set over a period from November 2020 to January 2021.

The Corona pandemic has created major disruptions to arts and culture practitioners, agencies and enterprises. While cultural institutions close we need new innovative approaches to continue our cultural exchange and fullfill the responsibility of education. Many arts and culture organizations already moved services online. The increasingly widespread adoption of technological innovations in the sector demonstrate that they could lead to more sustainable business models, wider access to arts and culture products and services, better networking and exchanges, and above all, a more sustainable environment of the sector. But, we need to do more and we need to develop ideas. So, let's get started together!

For the expected outcome of the hack, the applications or games shall be open source and use open data to tackle the climate change, environment and sustainable development challenges.

How does an ideathon work

At Ideathons participants develop ideas for projects that tackle a specific problem or area. Participants usually come from a variety of backgrounds and bring in unique perspectives. In UNESCO Techcul participants collaborate before the Ideathon event online on a platform and form teams. They also prepare for activities during the Ideathon event and research relevant topics. At the ideathon participants work together for the final submission which includes materials, presentations and videos about their idea. A few days after the online event the UNESCO selection team will announce the best ideas that will participate in a pitch event in January. An Ideathon is very similar to a Hackathon. In contrast to a Hackathon you focus more on the concept of a problem you would like to solve. It is a potential first step before developing an idea further into a technical solution or prototype. About UNESCO Techcul

UNESCO is launching TechCul to bring together tech and culture leaders with startups and entrepreneurs to develop win-win digital innovative technology solutions and business models to address the challenges faced in the culture and creative sector in times of a global pandemic.

The Corona pandemic has created major disruptions to arts and culture practitioners, agencies and enterprises. The closing of cultural institutions, collapse of existing businesses and decreasing of practitioners’ income and employment show that we need innovative approaches urgently. Many arts and culture organizations were already successful in moving services online or sped up the adoption of existing or new technology.

The increasingly widespread adoption of technological innovations and digital solutions in the culture and creativity sector demonstrate that they could lead to more sustainable business models, wider access to arts and culture products and services, better networking and exchanges, and above all, a more sustainable environment of the sector. So, let's work on this together!
